🥾 Zennor → Gurnard's Head coast path
~3 mi · about 2 hrs · rugged & scenic
⌄

Join the South West Coast Path at Zennor Head and head west, keeping the sea on your right. The path winds along dramatic granite clifftops above Pendour Cove and Mussel Point, dipping and climbing between coves — only ~3 miles but genuinely slow-going and rocky, so it fills a good 2 hours. Watch for seals, stonechats and diving gannets. At Gurnard's Head (a gurnard-fish-shaped promontory, worth a quick explore) turn inland and climb the short track up to the Gurnard's Head Hotel on the B3306.

Footwear matters: the path is uneven, worn and slippery over rock in places — proper walking shoes/boots, water and a snack. It's beautiful but not a stroll.

↩︎ If the coast path looks too rugged

The walk is short but genuinely rocky. For an easier 2 hours, walk the Zennor Churchway (Coffin Path) instead — an ancient, mostly-flat inland route through farmland that also links Zennor and Gurnard's Head. Same buses, same pub lunch, far gentler underfoot.

🦭 Seal Island sea safari
From the harbour · Atlantic grey seals, maybe dolphins
⌄

The classic St Ives outing and a brilliant one for a group. Boats leave the harbour throughout the day (Easter–Nov), heading ~3.5 miles west past Porthmeor and the Tate to Seal Island (The Carracks), where you watch the colony up close before cruising back. Around an hour, suitable for all ages, dogs go free. Several operators (St Ives Boats' "Dolly P", Bluefin/Yellowfin catamarans, the Little Mermaid) — just turn up at the harbour kiosks or pre-book.

Tip: trips depend on tide & weather, so check the day before. There's also a longer "Ultimate" safari (~9am) that pushes west past Gurnard's Head to Pendeen and over to Godrevy.

St Michael's Mount & Marazion
Castle open Fri · gardens close for the season today
⌄

Friday is actually the one day this trip the Mount works — the castle is open (it's shut Saturdays) and the gardens are open through 4 Sept, their last day of the season. Train St Ives→Penzance every ~30 min (20–40 min via St Erth), then a short bus/walk to Marazion. Cross the causeway at low tide or by boat at high tide. Allow 2–4 hrs on the island; pre-book a timed castle/garden ticket.

Check before you go: the causeway opening depends on the tide — use the Mount's "Causeway Calculator", and book castle + boat tickets online in advance (no tickets sold at the entrance in season).

🗿 Penwith Gallery
Back Road East · sculpture courtyard · free
⌄

A short walk from the Hepworth. 🏺 One more for ceramics fans
